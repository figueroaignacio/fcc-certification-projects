Linus Torvalds

Linus Torvalds is a Finnish software engineer who created the Linux operating system kernel and the distributed version control system Git. His contributions to open-source software have significantly influenced the world of technology.

Early Life and Education

  • 1969: Linus Benedict Torvalds is born on December 28 in Helsinki, Finland.
  • 1981: At the age of 11, Linus receives his first computer, a Commodore VIC-20, sparking his interest in programming.
  • 1987: Linus begins programming in assembly language on an IBM PC
  • 1988: Enrolls at the University of Helsinki to study computer science.

Linux Development and Early Recognition:

  • 1991:
    • Linus begins developing what would later become the Linux kernel as a hobby project.
    • On August 25, Linus posts his famous message on the comp.os.minix newsgroup announcing his project.
    • Version 0.01 of Linux is released in September.
  • 1992:
    • Linux is re-licensed under the GNU General Public License (GPL), aligning with the open-source movement.
    • Linux gains international attention as an alternative operating system to UNIX.
  • 1993: The Linux community grows rapidly, with more developers contributing to the project.
  • 1994: Linux kernel version 1.0 is officially released, marking its first stable release.

Professional Career and Expansion of Linux:

  • 1996:
    • The Linux kernel version 2.0 is released, supporting multiple processor architectures.
    • Linus selects the penguin as the official Linux mascot, later named "Tux."
  • 1997: Receives the 1997 EFF Pioneer Award for his contributions to open-source software.
  • 1998: The growing adoption of Linux leads major companies like IBM, Oracle, and Dell to support the operating system.
  • 1999: Linus moves to the United States and becomes a fellow at the Open Source Development Lab (OSDL).

Git and Continued Influence:

  • 2002: Linus and his family move to Portland, Oregon.
  • 2005:
    • Develops Git, a distributed version control system, in response to the proprietary nature of BitKeeper.
    • Git becomes the standard tool for version control in open-source and corporate projects.
  • 2007: Releases the Linux kernel version 2.6.23, introducing the Completely Fair Scheduler (CFS).

Modern Recognition and Ongoing Work:

  • 2010: Receives the IEEE Computer Society's Computer Pioneer Award.
  • 2012:
    • Awarded the Millennium Technology Prize by the Technology Academy Finland for creating Linux.
    • Inducted into the Internet Hall of Fame.
  • 2015: The Linux kernel celebrates its 24th birthday, with millions of devices worldwide running Linux.
  • 2018: Steps back temporarily from Linux kernel development to improve interpersonal behavior following criticism of his communication style.

Recent Years:

  • 2020: Oversees the release of Linux kernel version 5.8, one of the largest updates in Linux history.
  • 2022: Celebrates over 30 years of Linux development, which now powers everything from smartphones to supercomputers.
  • 2023: Continues his work on the Linux kernel, actively leading discussions and contributions from the global Linux development community.

Legacy and Contributions:

Linus Torvalds' work has transformed the technology industry. Linux powers over 90% of the world's supercomputers, most of the internet's servers, and billions of Android devices. Git is the foundation for collaborative software development globally. His commitment to open-source principles remains a cornerstone of his legacy.
